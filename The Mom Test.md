# The Mom Test - Core Notes

## The three core rules

The Mom Test is a method for asking questions that even your mom cannot lie about.
Rule 1: Talk about their life instead of your idea.
Rule 2: Ask about specifics in the past instead of generics or opinions about the future.
Rule 3: Talk less and listen more.

Good customer conversations focus on facts from real behavior.
Bad customer conversations focus on opinions, hypotheticals, and validation.

## Why compliments are fool's gold

Compliments are the fool's gold of customer learning: shiny, distracting, and entirely worthless.
People often say nice things to protect your feelings.
A compliment like "I love it" is not evidence of intent to buy.
The right move is to deflect compliments and return to concrete facts.

## How to anchor fluff

Fluff includes generic claims, future promises, and hypotheticals.
When someone says "I always" or "I would," ask for specific past examples.
A strong anchoring question is: "When was the last time that happened?"
Another anchoring question is: "Can you talk me through that situation?"
Anchoring turns vague statements into observable behavior data.
To anchor fluff, move from generic claims to concrete past behavior.
How do you anchor fluffy customer answers to concrete past behavior? Ask for the last real example and the exact workflow.

## What advancement looks like

A meeting succeeds when it ends with advancement or commitment.
Advancement means a clear next step in the buying process.
Commitment means they give up something valuable: time, reputation, or money.
Examples include: scheduling the next meeting, introducing a decision maker, starting a paid trial, or giving a deposit.
A meeting that ends with "Looks great, keep me posted" usually failed.
What signals show that a customer meeting has truly advanced? A concrete next step and a real commitment.

## What to do if you pitch too early

If you accidentally start pitching too soon, apologize immediately.
Say that you got excited and want to get back to their workflow.
Then return to questions about their current behavior and constraints.
The goal is to remove bias and recover truthful data.

## Additional practical cues

Ask: "Where does the money come from?" in B2B situations.
Ask: "Who else should I talk to?" at the end of each useful conversation.
If there is no clear next step, the meeting probably did not produce real progress.
If users are not actively searching for a solution, the problem may not be urgent enough.
